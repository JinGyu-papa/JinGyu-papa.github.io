Current Research Focus

  • Prime Prediction via Admissibility Selection.
    Develop a “readout” operator that predicts prime gaps by selecting the unique stable candidate from an admissible set (square–free filtering, small–factor screening, and head selection).
  • Branch–Selective Log–Phase Dynamics.
    Model prime progression as a relation–driven update on (u, θ, σ), where phase laws switch via a finite–state branch variable (selection, not generation).
  • Invariant Persistence and Scaling.
    Test whether the doublet and symmetry invariants remain invariant under scale extension, supporting their role as structural constraints rather than finite-size artifacts.
  • RH–to–Readout Interface (supporting track).
    Maintain a consistent “same observable” pipeline linking explicit–formula resonance constraints to structural mean–square control.

Research Principle

  • No guesswork: I only trust mechanisms that survive ablations (reference on/off, filter on/off, scaling stress tests).
  • Hard falsifiability: If the operator can’t generalize, the hypothesis is rejected.
  • Minimal assumptions, maximal structure: Remove known sources of coherence (squares / small-period artifacts) before claiming “laws.”
  • Implementation-first: A theory earns its right to exist only when it compiles into a working readout engine.
